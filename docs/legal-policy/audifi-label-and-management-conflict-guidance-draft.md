## 4. Common Restrictive Clauses

The following are common types of contract provisions that may affect AudiFi use. This list is NOT exhaustive.

### 4.1 Recording Contract Provisions

**Exclusive Recording Rights:**
- Many label contracts grant the label exclusive rights to your recordings
- This may prevent you from uploading or minting music you recorded under the contract
- Some contracts extend to all recordings during the contract term, not just label-produced recordings

**360 Deal Provisions:**
- Some contracts grant labels rights to income from multiple sources
- This may include merchandise, touring, licensing, and potentially NFT sales
- Revenue from Master IPOs might be claimed by labels under such provisions

**Reversion Clauses:**
- Some contracts specify when rights revert to artists
- Masters under label control may not be available for Master IPOs
- Check if rights have reverted before using older recordings

### 4.2 Publishing Agreement Provisions

**Controlled Composition Clauses:**
- May affect how royalties from your compositions are handled
- Could impact revenue distribution from Master IPOs

**Exclusive Publishing Rights:**
- Publishers may have rights in underlying compositions
- Compositions may require separate clearance from publishing rights

### 4.3 Management Agreement Provisions

**Commission Clauses:**
- Managers may be entitled to commissions on NFT income
- This could affect your net revenue from Master IPOs

**Approval Requirements:**
- Some management agreements require manager approval for business decisions
- Initiating a Master IPO might require consultation or approval

### 4.4 Distribution Agreement Provisions

**Exclusive Distribution Rights:**
- Distributors may have exclusive rights to distribute your music
- This could conflict with AudiFi marketplace distribution

**Platform Restrictions:**
- Some agreements restrict which platforms you can use
- Check if AudiFi or NFT platforms are addressed

### 4.5 Collaboration and Band Agreements

**Co-ownership Provisions:**
- If music was created collaboratively, multiple parties may have rights
- All rights holders may need to agree to Master IPO participation

**Revenue Sharing:**
- Existing agreements may require revenue sharing with collaborators
- Master IPO structures must account for such obligations

## 5. Steps Before Using AudiFi

### 5.1 Review Your Agreements

Before uploading content or initiating a Master IPO:

1. **Gather all relevant contracts** - Recording, publishing, management, distribution, collaboration agreements
2. **Read them carefully** - Look for exclusive rights, restrictions, and approval requirements
3. **Check key dates** - Note contract terms, reversion dates, and option periods
4. **Identify restrictions** - Note any provisions that might limit your use of AudiFi

### 5.2 Consult Legal Counsel

We strongly recommend:

1. **Hiring an entertainment attorney** who understands both traditional music contracts and digital/NFT matters
2. **Providing all relevant agreements** for review
3. **Asking specific questions** about your ability to use AudiFi for each piece of content
4. **Getting written advice** documenting your attorney's conclusions

### 5.3 Communicate with Your Team

If you have existing industry relationships:

1. **Discuss your plans** with your label, publisher, and/or manager
2. **Seek necessary permissions** if required by your agreements
3. **Document communications** and agreements in writing
4. **Clarify revenue treatment** for NFT income

### 5.4 Proceed with Caution

If there is any uncertainty:

1. **Do not upload content** until rights are clear
2. **Do not initiate Master IPOs** for disputed masters
3. **Seek explicit permission** rather than assuming rights
4. **Consider starting with unencumbered content** (self-produced, fully owned)

### 6.1 Platform Neutrality

AudiFi:
- Does NOT take sides in disputes between artists and their contractual counterparties
- Does NOT verify artist compliance with external agreements
- Does NOT adjudicate ownership or rights disputes

AudiFi intends to:
- Cooperate with valid legal process (court orders, subpoenas)

### 6.4 No Indemnification of Artists

AudiFi does NOT indemnify artists against claims arising from:
- Breach of their existing contracts
- Disputes with labels, publishers, or managers

## 7. If a Dispute Arises

### 7.1 What AudiFi May Do

If AudiFi receives complaints or legal claims regarding your content:

- We may notify you of the claim
- We may request documentation of your rights
- We may disable or remove content pending resolution
- We may delist NFTs from marketplace displays
- We may cooperate with legal process

### 7.2 Your Options

If your content is affected by a dispute:

- Consult with your attorney
- Pursue informal resolution with the complainant
- Submit counter-notifications where applicable
- Initiate legal proceedings if appropriate

### 7.3 Resolution Is Your Responsibility

AudiFi does NOT:
- Mediate disputes
- Determine who has superior rights
- Provide legal support to artists
- Take sides in conflicts

Resolution of disputes is the responsibility of the parties involved.
