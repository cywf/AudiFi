## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This Artist Rights & Ownership Responsibility Policy ("Policy") describes the responsibilities of artists who use the AudiFi platform to upload content, participate in V Studio sessions, or initiate Master IPOs and mint NFTs representing shares in Master revenue.

### 1.2 Scope

This Policy applies to all users who:
- Upload audio, visual, or other creative content to AudiFi
- Initiate a Master IPO for any Master recording
- Mint NFTs representing revenue shares in Masters
- Participate in V Studio as a content creator or session host
- Use any Artist Coin or access pass creation features

### 1.3 Relationship to Terms of Service

This Policy is incorporated by reference into the AudiFi Terms of Service. By accepting the Terms of Service and using the platform as an artist, you agree to comply with this Policy.

## 2. Artist Representations and Warranties

### 2.1 Content Ownership and Rights

By uploading content to AudiFi or initiating a Master IPO, you represent and warrant that:

1. **Ownership or Authorization:** You either:
   - Own all rights, title, and interest in and to the content (including all underlying compositions, recordings, samples, and elements), OR
   - Have obtained all necessary licenses, permissions, and authorizations from all rights holders to use the content on AudiFi for the purposes described in the Artist Minting & Master IPO Terms

2. **No Infringement:** Your use of the content on AudiFi does not and will not infringe, violate, or misappropriate any third party's:
   - Copyright or related rights
   - Trademark or service mark rights
   - Rights of publicity or privacy
   - Trade secrets or confidential information
   - Any other intellectual property or proprietary rights

3. **No Conflicting Obligations:** Your use of the content on AudiFi, including initiating a Master IPO, does not breach any:
   - Existing recording contract
   - Publishing agreement
   - Management agreement
   - Distribution agreement
   - Licensing agreement
   - Any other contractual obligation to a third party

4. **Sample and Interpolation Clearance:** If your content contains samples, interpolations, or elements from third-party works, you have obtained all necessary clearances and licenses for such use

5. **Performer and Contributor Rights:** You have obtained all necessary consents, releases, and agreements from:
   - All performers appearing in or contributing to the content
   - All session musicians, vocalists, and collaborators
   - Any other individuals with rights in the content

### 2.2 Authority to Initiate Master IPO

By initiating a Master IPO and minting NFTs representing shares in Master revenue, you additionally represent and warrant that:

1. **Full Authority:** You have the full legal authority to:
   - Designate the Master for a Master IPO
   - Grant the rights associated with NFT ownership as described in the platform documentation
   - Receive and distribute revenue in accordance with the smart contract mechanics

2. **No Encumbrances:** The Master is not subject to any liens, claims, or encumbrances that would prevent or limit the grant of rights to NFT holders

3. **Accurate Information:** All information you provide about the Master, including metadata, ownership information, and revenue projections, is accurate and complete to the best of your knowledge

### 2.3 V Studio Participation

If you host or participate in V Studio sessions as a creator, you represent and warrant that:

1. **Performance Rights:** You have the right to perform any content during the session
2. **No Unauthorized Disclosures:** You will not disclose confidential information belonging to third parties
3. **Compliance with Guidelines:** You will comply with all applicable community guidelines and session rules

## 3. Platform's Role and Limitations

### 3.1 Technical Infrastructure Provider

AudiFi provides technical infrastructure, including:
- Smart contract deployment for Master IPO shares (ERC-721C)
- Royalty distribution mechanics (ERC-2981 integration, Mover Advantage allocations)
- V Studio session hosting and streaming capabilities
- Dashboards for tracking revenue and NFT performance
- Artist Coin (ERC-20) and access pass (ERC-1155C) functionality

**AudiFi does NOT:**
- Provide legal vetting, clearance, or verification of artist rights claims
- Guarantee that artists have the rights they claim to have
- Become a party to any agreement between artists and their labels, publishers, managers, or other third parties
- Act as a mediator or adjudicator in disputes between artists and third parties
- Provide legal, tax, or financial advice

### 3.2 No Legal Vetting

AudiFi does not review, verify, or validate:
- Artist ownership claims
- License or permission documentation
- Contractual relationships between artists and third parties
- Sample clearances or interpolation licenses
- Performer or contributor releases

**Artists are solely responsible for ensuring they have all necessary rights and permissions.**

### 3.3 No Guarantees

AudiFi makes no guarantees regarding:
- The accuracy of information provided by artists
- The validity of rights claims made by artists
- The enforceability of smart contracts in any jurisdiction
- Revenue projections or financial outcomes
- Secondary market liquidity or pricing

## 4. Label, Publisher, and Management Conflicts

### 4.1 Existing Agreements

If you have existing agreements with:
- Record labels
- Music publishers
- Artist managers
- Distribution companies
- Licensing agencies
- Any other music industry entities

You are strongly encouraged to:

1. **Review Your Agreements:** Carefully review all existing agreements before using AudiFi
2. **Consult Legal Counsel:** Seek advice from a qualified entertainment attorney regarding your contractual obligations
3. **Obtain Necessary Permissions:** If required by your agreements, obtain written permission before uploading content or initiating a Master IPO
4. **Communicate with Your Team:** Discuss your plans with your label, publisher, or management to avoid misunderstandings

### 4.2 Common Restrictive Clauses

Artists should be aware that existing agreements may contain provisions regarding:
- Exclusive recording rights
- Restrictions on self-release or alternative distribution
- Revenue sharing or accounting obligations
- Approval requirements for new releases
- Right of first refusal clauses
- Territory or platform restrictions

**This list is not exhaustive. Consult your own legal counsel for guidance specific to your situation.**

### 4.3 Platform's Neutrality

AudiFi:
- Does not take sides in disputes between artists and their contractual counterparties
- Does not verify whether artists have complied with existing agreements
- Does not provide guidance on whether specific agreements permit AudiFi use

### 4.4 Consequences of Breach

If you breach your contractual obligations to third parties by using AudiFi:
- You are solely responsible for any resulting liability
- AudiFi may be required to comply with lawful legal processes
- Your content may be subject to takedown or removal
- Your account may be subject to sanctions
