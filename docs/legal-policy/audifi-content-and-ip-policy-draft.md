## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This Content & Intellectual Property Policy ("Content Policy") establishes the content standards, prohibited content categories, and notice-and-takedown procedures for the AudiFi platform.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All content uploaded to AudiFi
- All NFTs minted on the platform
- All user-generated content (profiles, comments, descriptions)
- All V Studio session content

### 1.3 Relationship to Terms of Service

This Content Policy is incorporated by reference into the AudiFi Terms of Service. Violation of this policy may result in content removal, account suspension, or other enforcement actions.

## 2. User Content Responsibilities

### 2.1 Ownership and Rights

Users uploading content to AudiFi must:

1. **Own or License Rights:** Either own the content or have all necessary licenses to use it on AudiFi
2. **Clear All Elements:** Have proper clearance for samples, interpolations, and third-party elements
3. **Obtain Consents:** Have releases from all performers, collaborators, and contributors
4. **Verify Accuracy:** Ensure all metadata and descriptions are accurate

### 2.2 Ongoing Responsibility

Content uploaders:
- Remain responsible for content throughout its presence on the platform
- Must update or remove content if circumstances change
- Must respond to legitimate inquiries about their rights
- Must cooperate with rights holder inquiries

See the Rights & Ownership Responsibility Policy for detailed obligations.

## 3. Prohibited Content

### 3.1 Intellectual Property Violations

The following content is prohibited:

1. **Infringing Content:** Content that infringes copyrights, trademarks, or other intellectual property rights
2. **Uncleared Samples:** Audio containing samples without proper licenses
3. **Unauthorized Covers:** Cover versions without mechanical licenses where required
4. **Stolen Content:** Content uploaded without the creator's permission
5. **Counterfeit Items:** NFTs falsely claiming to represent particular content or artists

### 3.2 Illegal Content

The following content is prohibited:

1. **Illegal Material:** Content that violates any applicable law
2. **Child Exploitation:** Any material involving the exploitation of minors
3. **Terrorism Content:** Material promoting terrorism or violent extremism
4. **Regulated Goods:** Content promoting illegal sale of weapons, drugs, or contraband
5. **Criminal Activity:** Content facilitating fraud, hacking, or other crimes

### 3.3 Harmful Content

The following content is prohibited:

1. **Hate Speech:** Content promoting hatred against protected groups
2. **Harassment:** Content that harasses, threatens, or intimidates individuals
3. **Violence:** Gratuitous violence or content encouraging harm to others
4. **Defamation:** False statements that harm others' reputations
5. **Privacy Violations:** Content that invades others' privacy without consent

### 3.4 Misleading Content

The following content is prohibited:

1. **Impersonation:** Content falsely claiming to be from particular artists or entities
2. **Fraudulent Claims:** False or misleading descriptions of content or rights
3. **Fake Endorsements:** False claims of endorsement or affiliation
4. **Misleading Metadata:** Inaccurate or deceptive metadata

### 3.5 Platform Abuse

The following is prohibited:

1. **Spam:** Repetitive, bulk, or commercial spam content
2. **Manipulation:** Fake engagement, artificial inflation of metrics
3. **Scams:** Content designed to defraud other users
4. **Malware:** Content containing or distributing malicious code

## 4. Notice-and-Takedown Procedure

### 4.1 Overview

AudiFi maintains a notice-and-takedown procedure for addressing claims of intellectual property infringement, modeled on the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) process.

*[Note to Counsel: This section should be adapted to applicable jurisdictions and specific legal requirements.]*

### 4.2 Submitting a Takedown Notice

Rights holders who believe their work is being infringed may submit a takedown notice. A valid notice must include:

1. **Identification of Work:** Identification of the copyrighted work(s) claimed to be infringed

2. **Identification of Infringing Material:** Identification of the material claimed to be infringing, with sufficient information to locate it (e.g., URLs)

3. **Contact Information:** Contact information for the complaining party, including:
   - Name
   - Address
   - Telephone number
   - Email address

4. **Good Faith Statement:** A statement that the complaining party has a good faith belief that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law

5. **Accuracy Statement:** A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that the complaining party is authorized to act on behalf of the owner

6. **Signature:** A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized representative

Takedown notices should be sent to:

DMCA Agent: [Designated Agent Name]  
Address: [Address Placeholder]  
Email: [DMCA Email Placeholder]  

### 4.4 AudiFi's Response to Valid Notices

Upon receipt of a valid takedown notice, AudiFi may:

1. **Remove or Disable Access:** Promptly remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material

2. **Notify Uploader:** Notify the user who uploaded the content of the takedown and provide a copy of the notice

3. **Delist NFTs:** Delist or restrict NFTs associated with infringing content

4. **Provide Counter-Notice Information:** Inform the uploader of the counter-notice procedure

### 4.5 Counter-Notification Procedure

If you believe content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notification including:

1. **Identification of Material:** Identification of the material that was removed and its prior location

2. **Contact Information:** Your name, address, telephone number, and email address

3. **Consent to Jurisdiction:** A statement consenting to jurisdiction of [Jurisdiction Placeholder] and that you will accept service of process from the party who filed the original notice

4. **Good Faith Statement:** A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification

5. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature

### 4.6 Response to Counter-Notices

Upon receipt of a valid counter-notification, AudiFi may:

1. Forward the counter-notification to the original complainant
2. Inform the complainant that material may be restored in [10-14] business days unless the complainant initiates legal action
3. Restore access to the material after the waiting period if no court order is obtained

## 6. Enforcement Actions

### 6.1 Available Actions

AudiFi reserves the right to take enforcement actions including:

1. **Content Removal:** Removing or disabling access to content
2. **NFT Delisting:** Delisting NFTs from marketplace displays
3. **Account Warnings:** Issuing warnings to users
4. **Account Suspension:** Temporarily suspending account access
5. **Account Termination:** Permanently terminating accounts
6. **Referral to Authorities:** Reporting violations to law enforcement where appropriate

### 6.2 Discretionary Enforcement

AudiFi enforces this policy at its discretion. We are not obligated to:

- Monitor all content proactively
- Take action on every potential violation
- Follow a specific escalation path
- Provide detailed explanations for enforcement decisions

### 6.3 Appeal Process

Users subject to enforcement actions may:

- Contact support to request reconsideration
- Provide additional information or context
- Submit counter-notifications where applicable

Appeals are reviewed but AudiFi's decisions are final.
