## 3. Understanding Master IPO NFTs

### 3.1 What NFTs Represent

NFTs representing Master IPO shares are digital assets that:
- Are recorded on the blockchain (ERC-721C standard)
- May entitle holders to revenue distributions as encoded in the smart contract
- Include royalty mechanics (ERC-2981 integration)
- May confer certain access rights or benefits on the platform

### 3.2 What NFTs Do NOT Represent

Master IPO NFTs do NOT necessarily represent:

1. **Copyright Ownership:** Holding an NFT does not transfer ownership of the copyright in the underlying Master recording. The artist retains intellectual property rights unless explicitly stated otherwise.

2. **Traditional Securities:** The characterization of NFTs under securities laws is complex and subject to regulatory interpretation. Consult your own legal advisors.

3. **Guaranteed Returns:** NFTs are not guaranteed to appreciate in value, generate revenue, or maintain any value.

4. **Debt or Equity Instruments:** NFTs are not traditional debt obligations or equity interests in any entity.

### 3.3 Final Rights Description

The specific rights associated with any NFT are described in:
- Platform documentation at the time of purchase
- Smart contract code and metadata
- Artist-provided descriptions

The final characterization and legal nature of these rights is subject to detailed legal review and may vary by jurisdiction. You should consult qualified legal counsel regarding the rights you acquire.
